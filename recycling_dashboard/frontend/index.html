Why Recycling Matters

Recycling plays a vital role in reducing environmental pollution and conserving natural resources. This dashboard analyzes recycling rates across materials (Plastic, Paper, Glass, Metal), US states, and OECD countries — revealing how policy, infrastructure, and awareness drive dramatically different outcomes.

8.7%Plastic recycling rate in the US (2018) — the lowest of all tracked materials, despite plastic being the most visible waste issue.
68.2%Paper recycling rate in the US (2018) — the highest material rate, driven by decades of curbside programs.
91.4%Glass recycling rate in Michigan — bottle deposit laws make a massive difference vs states with no deposit.
54%South Korea's overall MSW recycling rate — one of the highest globally, driven by mandatory sorting laws.

Key Findings

Plastic crisisDespite 60 years of data, plastic recycling has never exceeded 10% in the US — far behind all other tracked materials.
Policy mattersStates with bottle deposit laws (Michigan, Oregon, NY) recycle 3–6× more glass and metal than states without them.
Global gapThe US ranks 30th globally in waste recovery. Germany and South Korea recycle roughly twice as much municipal waste.
Paper successPaper recycling grew from 17% in 1960 to 68% in 2018 — proof that infrastructure investment drives real change.
Regional divideTexas plastic rate (4.1%) vs California (16.5%) — regulation explains most of the 4× difference between states.
1960 baselineAll materials started below 5% in 1960. 58 years of data show what policy, technology, and awareness can achieve.
